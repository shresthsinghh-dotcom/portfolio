Adaptive Ball Launcher for Accessible Independent Play

This project focused on the design and prototyping of an adaptive, spring-powered ball launcher intended for children with motor impairments, including cerebral palsy. Many existing solutions are either prohibitively expensive, overly complex, or unsafe, limiting opportunities for independent play and therapeutic engagement. The goal was to create a low-cost, durable, and easily replicable device that enables children to launch objects with minimal force while maintaining safety and accessibility.

The team employed a human-centered engineering design process, working with pediatric occupational therapists and caregivers to define clear quantitative design criteria, including launch distance, adaptability to different projectiles, durability, modularity, and ease of use. The final design used a spring-actuated linear drive system housed in a modular frame with a universal mounting option, allowing the launcher to be freestanding or wheelchair-mounted.

Extensive testing was conducted to evaluate performance against the design criteria, including launch distance, safety features, and user adaptability. The prototype successfully met the established criteria, demonstrating effective launch distances with various projectiles while ensuring user safety through controlled power settings and protective barriers. The modular design allowed for easy adjustments and maintenance, making it suitable for diverse user needs.
